Path

Phased Path for DriveTime

A pragmatic rollout: start broad with an assistant, deepen with agents, then standardize on the winning fabric.
Blueprint & Design
Phase 0 – Align & scope
  • Exec workshop across Corporate, IT, Contact Centers, Retail.
  • Define KPIs, success criteria, and guardrails (security, data residency).
  • Identify systems and data for initial rollout.
Launch
Phase 1 – Assistant & first agents
  • Connect core systems; deploy **DriveTime‑wide Glean assistant**.
  • Stand up 1–2 focused agents (e.g., Contact Center, HR/IT).
  • Start measuring time‑saved, deflection, AHT, and adoption.
Scale
Phase 2 – Expand & standardize
  • Extend agents across brands and workflows.
  • Compare impact vs existing point tools (Copilot, Secoda, bespoke bots).
  • Make platform standardization and tool rationalization decisions.

Executive Takeaways

The question is less “if AI” and more “on what fabric, for which outcomes, and how fast.”
  • 1 DriveTime already has the ambition, mandate, and leadership to be AI‑first; the missing piece is a unified Work AI platform that spans Corporate, Contact Centers, IT, and Retail.
  • 2 Glean is designed to fill that role: connect enterprise data once, govern centrally, and serve many use cases via assistants and agents, backed by results at peers like T‑Mobile and CarGurus.
  • 3 A pragmatic path starts with an enterprise assistant plus a small set of high‑impact agents, demonstrating in‑year cost savings and productivity gains, then standardizing on the Work AI fabric that proves itself.
